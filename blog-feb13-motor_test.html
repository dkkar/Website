Motor Testing!

Posted on February 13, 2020 by Mauris Winata

Various interations of the carriage were designed. The first design can be seen below:

    In order to test the motors, a controller is required. For this purpose, an Arduino is utilised. The Arduino sends signals to an ESC which then controls the BLDC motor. As the motor operates on 24V, a 24V AC/DC converter is used. The motor is soldered to the ESC which is soldered to the power source. Sanjay can be seen assisting in the soldering.

After soldering, the motor can be tested:

The gimbal motor controller will be dependent on accelerometer readings. As such, in this test set-up, a Bosch BNO055 is connected to the Arduino. The motor speed and direction can then be controlled by tilting the breadboard at various angles.
